52:["$","h3",null,{"children":"4.3 Symbiogenesis as a productive metaphor"}]
53:["$","p",null,{"children":"The symbiogenesis metaphor — genomes as ecosystems of co-inherited modules — evolved through three phases during this project. Initially, we treated all k-mers as \"species\" in an ecosystem and applied ecological metrics (Bray-Curtis, Morisita-Horn, Shannon diversity). This produced the k-mer ecosystem and diversity index algorithms, which work on GIAB but fail on CEPH because the \"ecosystem\" of all k-mers is 98% identical across all individuals."}]
54:["$","p",null,{"children":["The metaphor became productive when refined to focus on ",["$","em",null,{"children":"rare variant modules"}]," — clusters of linked rare k-mers that represent inherited haplotype blocks. In this framing, haplotype continuity measures the \"persistence\" of a rare variant community: how many consecutive rare k-mers survive from parent to child. Related individuals share long-lived variant communities; unrelated individuals share only transient, scattered variants."]}]
55:["$","p",null,{"children":"The extended metaphor — structural variation as \"mobile cargo\" between genomic \"hosts\" — is biologically apt (mobile elements are literally parasitic DNA that carries cargo) but practically insufficient on short reads. The cargo transfer algorithm captures the right idea but cannot see enough of the mobile element to make it work for kinship detection."}]
56:["$","p",null,{"children":["Where the symbiogenesis metaphor helps most: it encourages thinking about ",["$","em",null,{"children":"groups"}]," of co-inherited markers rather than individual markers, and about ",["$","em",null,{"children":"continuity"}]," rather than presence/absence. This directly led to the run-length insight and the rare-variant refinement. Where it misleads: the bulk genome structure is too conserved to be a useful \"ecosystem,\" and the metaphor sometimes suggests looking at macro-scale structural features (SVs, VNTRs) that are too sparse for short-read kinship detection."]}]
57:["$","h3",null,{"children":"4.4 SV ecology: promise and limitations"}]
58:["$","p",null,{"children":["The SV ecology results reveal a fundamental distinction: ",["$","strong",null,{"children":"population-informative does not imply kinship-informative"}],".
